# Cluster 4: Transformation & Wisdom
## Sources: Hesse (Siddhartha, Steppenwolf), Rilke, Drummond, Galeano

---

## SCENARIO 1: The River
**Escalation Level:** Medium

**Scene Setup:**
You stand at the edge of a river. The water carries fragments -- voices, faces, things you once wanted. Everything passes. Nothing stops.

**The Choice:**
"I step into the current." / "I stay on the bank and watch."

- **Choice A -- Step in:** The visitor trusts dissolution. Willing to lose footing, to let identity blur at the edges. Readiness to participate in what cannot be controlled -- or a compulsion to merge, to vanish into movement rather than bear stillness.

- **Choice B -- Stay and watch:** The visitor holds the position of witness. Can tolerate seeing without possessing. This may be wisdom -- or the old defense: staying dry while life passes.

**Source:** Siddhartha -- the river as teacher, Vasudeva the ferryman who only listens, the moment Siddhartha stops seeking and begins hearing.

**Devolucao:** The Oracle notes whether the visitor seeks experience through immersion or observation. Do they need to be inside the thing to trust it? Or distance to feel safe?

---

## SCENARIO 2: The Door That Costs Everything
**Escalation Level:** Deep

**Scene Setup:**
A door appears. Above it, scratched into the wood: "The price of entry is your certainty." Behind you, the path is familiar. Ahead, you hear laughter -- but you cannot tell if it is kind.

**The Choice:**
"I open the door." / "I listen longer before deciding."

- **Choice A -- Open:** Willing to trade what they know for what they don't. Accept the cost -- or don't fully believe the price is real. Either genuine courage, or the impulsiveness of someone who has never been truly charged for entering.

- **Choice B -- Listen longer:** Not refusing, but not surrendering. Want more information before the leap. Either the deepest respect for what's at stake, or the habit of someone who uses deliberation as a way to never quite arrive.

**Source:** Steppenwolf -- the Magic Theater ("Price of admission: your mind"), Pablo's laughter.

**Devolucao:** Some visitors pay every toll immediately; others negotiate with every threshold. The Oracle reads this as two different relationships with loss.

---

## SCENARIO 3: The Unanswerable
**Escalation Level:** Profound

**Scene Setup:**
The darkness thins. A question forms -- not in words, but as pressure in the chest. You feel it clearly, though you could not speak it. It has no answer. It may never have one.

**The Choice:**
"I carry the question with me." / "I let the question dissolve."

- **Choice A -- Carry:** Accepts the burden of not-knowing as companion. Profound tolerance for ambiguity -- or the grip of someone who hoards even suffering because emptiness is worse.

- **Choice B -- Let dissolve:** Trusts that not everything must be carried. Allows something to exist without possessing it. Either the deepest form of surrender -- or practiced forgetting of someone who abandons what is difficult before it can teach them.

**Source:** Rilke -- "Live the questions themselves." Patience with the unsolved.

**Devolucao:** This is the game's deepest mirror. Read against everything before. A visitor who consistently chose action and now carries the unanswerable reveals a shift. A visitor who consistently chose distance and now lets it go reveals another kind of shift. The devolucao is about what changed.

## SCENARIO 4: The Stone
**Escalation Level:** Light (deceptively)

**Scene Setup:**
The path opens into plain light. Nothing extraordinary. Ordinary ground, ordinary air. In the middle of the path: a stone. Not remarkable. Not symbolic. Just a stone.

**The Choice:**
"I pick it up." / "I walk past it."

- **Choice A -- Pick up:** Finds significance in what is simply there. Willing to be touched by the ordinary. Either genuine openness to the sacred-in-the-mundane -- or the anxious habit of extracting meaning from everything, unable to let a stone be a stone.

- **Choice B -- Walk past:** Lets the ordinary remain ordinary. Freedom from the need to charge the world with meaning. Or the blindness of someone who walks past everything that doesn't announce itself loudly enough.

**Source:** Drummond -- "No meio do caminho tinha uma pedra." The reverse side of things.

**Devolucao:** Placed late in the game, after heavier choices, it reveals how the visitor treats the absence of drama. Some are relieved by simplicity; others dismiss it. The Oracle reads this as the visitor's relationship with "enough."

---

## SCENARIO 5: The Other
**Escalation Level:** Deep

**Scene Setup:**
Someone is sitting in the dark ahead of you. You cannot see their face. They are not waiting for you -- they are simply there, alone, the way you are alone. They haven't noticed you yet.

**The Choice:**
"I sit beside them." / "I remain where I am."

- **Choice A -- Sit beside:** Moves toward the other. Shared solitude over private solitude. Either the deep recognition that aloneness is not the same as isolation -- or inability to tolerate separateness.

- **Choice B -- Remain:** Holds their own ground. Respects the other's solitude by not interrupting. Or their own by not abandoning it. Either dignity of letting two solitudes coexist -- or the old wall that looks like respect but is built from fear.

**Source:** Galeano -- solidarity vs charity, shared presence without transaction. Rilke -- love as "two solitudes that protect and border and greet each other."

**Devolucao:** The Oracle mirrors the visitor's pattern around others across the game. Have they moved toward or away? "You tend toward..." or "You tend away from..." and lets the visitor feel the weight.

## SCENARIO 6: The End of the Game
**Escalation Level:** Profound (final choice)

**Scene Setup:**
The Oracle's voice thins. The space grows quiet -- not empty, but finished. Something in you knows this is the last moment.

**The Choice:**
"I want to hear what you see in me." / "I already know what I need to know."

- **Choice A -- Ask for mirror:** Genuine openness to being reflected, willingness to be surprised by how one appears from outside -- or the hunger of someone who cannot validate their own experience, who needs authority to confirm what they already feel.

- **Choice B -- Already know:** Trusts their own interior. The deepest arrival the game can produce -- someone who has played, felt, and now owns their experience. Or the final defense: refusing the reflection because being truly seen is more frightening than never knowing.

**Source:** Siddhartha -- Govinda finally sees Siddhartha's face and it contains a thousand faces. Also Steppenwolf -- "You have a dimension too many."

**Devolucao:** Meta-choice. If A: Oracle delivers full devolucao. If B: "Then you already have what you came for." Brief, warm closure. The visitor's choice reveals their final relationship with the game itself: did they come here to be read, or to read themselves?

---

## CROSS-SCENARIO AXES

**Axis 1: Movement vs Stillness**
- Movement: step in, open door, carry question, pick up stone, sit beside, ask for mirror
- Stillness: stay on bank, listen longer, let dissolve, walk past, remain, already know

**Axis 2: Toward vs Inward**
- Toward: the current, the door, sitting beside, asking for mirror
- Inward: the bank, listening alone, remaining, declining mirror

Most interesting visitors will be inconsistent. The Oracle should name the break: "You held still through everything -- and then, at the last moment, you moved. What changed?"
