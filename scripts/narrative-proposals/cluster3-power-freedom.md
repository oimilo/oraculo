# Cluster 3: Power, Control & Freedom
## Sources: Orwell, Huxley, Shakespeare (Hamlet, Macbeth), Milton

---

## SCENARIO 1: The Comfortable Prison
**Escalation Level:** Light

**Scene Setup:**
You are in a room where everything adjusts to your preference. The temperature, the light, the sound -- all perfect. You have never suffered here. But you notice something: there is no door.

**The Choice:**
"I stay. I've never needed a door." / "I look for a way out."

- **Choice A -- Stay:** The visitor gravitates toward comfort even at the cost of agency. A willingness to trade freedom for the absence of friction. Safety purchased with sovereignty.

- **Choice B -- Look for exit:** The visitor experiences comfort itself as suspicious. Pleasure without choice is not pleasure at all, but anesthesia. A relationship with discomfort as proof of being alive.

**Source:** Brave New World (soma, pleasure as prison) + 1984 (controlled environment as invisible cage)

**Devolucao:** "You recognized the room immediately, didn't you? The one that gives you everything except the right to leave. Some people build that room themselves -- and call it peace."

## SCENARIO 2: The Voice That Knows
**Escalation Level:** Medium

**Scene Setup:**
There is a voice that has always lived inside you. It knows everything you have done. Everything you have hidden. Now it speaks -- not to punish you, but to correct you. It says: "I only want what is best for you."

**The Choice:**
"I listen. It knows me better than I know myself." / "I silence it."

- **Choice A -- Listen:** The visitor delegates authority over the self to an internal surveillance. Relief in being watched, in not having to decide alone. Deep entanglement between love and submission.

- **Choice B -- Silence:** The visitor rejects the voice. Tolerating the void it leaves -- the terrifying freedom of having no one to tell you who to be. Someone who has sensed that guidance can become a leash.

**Source:** 1984 (Big Brother as internalized surveillance, thoughtcrime) + Hamlet (the Ghost's command)

**Devolucao:** "The voice that corrects you -- you trusted it, or you fought it. But did you notice? Either way, you never questioned whether it was yours."

## SCENARIO 3: The Dagger and the Throne
**Escalation Level:** Medium

**Scene Setup:**
You are standing before something you have always wanted. It is finally within reach. But you can see, clearly, what it will cost. Not to others -- to you. A part of you that will not survive the taking.

**The Choice:**
"I take it." / "I walk away."

- **Choice A -- Take:** The visitor accepts transformation as a kind of mutilation. Desire demands sacrifice. Willingness to be consumed by one's own ambition, an intimate familiarity with the cost of wanting.

- **Choice B -- Walk away:** The visitor preserves self over desire. But renunciation has its own seduction: the moral high ground, the identity of the one who chose not to. Walking away can be its own form of power.

**Source:** Macbeth ("Is this a dagger which I see before me," ambition as self-destruction) + Paradise Lost (the cost of reaching for what is forbidden)

**Devolucao:** "What you wanted was right there. Whether you took it or turned away -- you already knew the price. The question was never whether you could afford it. It was whether you could afford to keep wanting."

## SCENARIO 4: The Fruit and the Garden
**Escalation Level:** Deep

**Scene Setup:**
You live in a place where every question has already been answered. Nothing is uncertain. Nothing is mysterious. Then someone offers you a single doubt -- small, sharp, irreversible. Once you hold it, the answers will stop making sense.

**The Choice:**
"I take the doubt." / "I keep the certainty."

- **Choice A -- Take doubt:** Knowledge over innocence. But also a hunger, a compulsion toward the forbidden disguised as curiosity. Cannot tolerate the paradise of not-knowing. Rather suffer in clarity than rest in blindness.

- **Choice B -- Keep certainty:** Not ignorance -- a decision to preserve meaning even at the cost of truth. Someone who understands that some knowledge does not liberate. It imprisons.

**Source:** Paradise Lost (Eve's choice, the Fall) + Brave New World (the Savage's confrontation with a world without mystery)

**Devolucao:** "You were offered the one thing that would ruin everything you understood. And your answer told me something: whether you reach for fire, or whether you have already been burned."

---

## SCENARIO 5: The Clean Hands
**Escalation Level:** Deep

**Scene Setup:**
You wake in the middle of the night. Your hands are moving on their own -- scrubbing, rubbing, over and over. You don't remember what you touched. But your hands remember.

**The Choice:**
"I stop them. I look at what they are trying to erase." / "I let them finish. Some things are better forgotten."

- **Choice A -- Stop and look:** Faith that looking at guilt transforms it. But the one who stares at the stain sometimes falls in love with it. Guilt, examined too closely, can become identity.

- **Choice B -- Let them finish:** Permitting the ritual. The body has its own intelligence, its own mercy. Not denial -- negotiation with the unbearable.

**Source:** Macbeth (Lady Macbeth's sleepwalking, "Out, damned spot") + Hamlet (the impossibility of clean action)

**Devolucao:** "Your hands knew something your mind refused. The stain was already inside. The question was only whether you would carry it as a wound or a secret."

## SCENARIO 6: The Last Translation
**Escalation Level:** Profound

**Scene Setup:**
You are given a language -- old, dense, yours. Every word holds a feeling no other word can hold. Now someone offers to simplify it. Fewer words. Less pain. Less confusion. But also: less of everything that has no name.

**The Choice:**
"Simplify it. I am tired of words I cannot explain." / "I keep every word. Even the ones that hurt."

- **Choice A -- Simplify:** Exhaustion with complexity, the burden of carrying experiences that resist articulation. What cannot be named cannot ambush you. Protecting oneself from inner life that refuses to behave.

- **Choice B -- Keep every word:** Refusal to be made smaller than one's experience. But keeping every wound in its original dialect, untranslated, unmetabolized. May confuse suffering with depth.

**Source:** 1984 (Newspeak -- destruction of language to limit thought) + Paradise Lost (the cost of consciousness)

**Devolucao:** "The words you are most afraid to lose are the ones that have no translation. They live in you like rooms no one else can enter."
